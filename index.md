<section id="strategy" class="section-card">
  <h2>The Min-Max Strategy</h2>
  <p class="section-intro">If you only remember three things, remember these: arrive earlier than feels necessary, move with intention in the first hour, and use the app constantly instead of reacting late.</p>

  <div class="grid cols-3">
    <div class="card accent-gold">
      <h3>1. Prep the day before</h3>
      <p>Choose your park, download the official app, connect everyone’s tickets, and decide your rope-drop targets in advance.</p>
      <ul>
        <li>If your hotel, corporate event, or ticket bundle includes early entry, use it.</li>
        <li>Create a group in the app so one person can help manage plans for everybody.</li>
        <li>Pick one “must hit,” one “nice to hit,” and one backup attraction.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="card accent-land">
      <h3>2. Win the first hour</h3>
      <p>Showing up at the gate early feels annoying, but it pays for itself fast. Queue outside once so you do not queue everywhere later.</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Aim to arrive well before opening.</li>
        <li>Once scanned in, start booking from the app immediately.</li>
        <li>Walk toward your first ride while someone in your group handles the phone work.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="card accent-sea">
      <h3>3. Stack systems, not stress</h3>
      <p>Think in layers: standby line, one app-based attraction benefit, one show request, and one food pickup plan.</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Check return times before tapping too fast.</li>
        <li>Earlier return windows usually keep your day more flexible.</li>
        <li>Set timers so you know when to check for your next opportunity.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section-card">
  <h2>Know the App Language</h2>
  <p class="section-intro">A lot of old advice online still says “FastPass.” Tokyo Disney Resort works differently now, so here is the simplified translation.</p>

  <div class="grid cols-2">
    <div class="card accent-gold">
      <h3>Disney Premier Access</h3>
      <p>Paid. Lets you select eligible attractions or viewing areas with reduced waiting. Best used on high-demand rides where time savings are real.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="card accent-land">
      <h3>40th Anniversary Priority Pass</h3>
      <p>Free. Available for eligible attractions after park entry. Great for protecting time later in the morning without paying.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="card accent-sea">
      <h3>Standby Pass</h3>
      <p>Free. Sometimes required for specific rides, shops, or areas depending on the day. If something requires it, the app is the source of truth.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="card accent-gold">
      <h3>Entry Request</h3>
      <p>Free. Used for certain shows and greetings. Availability and process can change, so treat the app as final rather than old blog posts.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="card accent-land">
      <h3>Disney Mobile Order</h3>
      <p>The lunch hack. Reserve a pickup slot before peak meal chaos, then confirm when you are nearby and skip the slowest part of the line.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="card accent-sea">
      <h3>Priority Seating</h3>
      <p>Advance dining reservations for select restaurants. Useful if you want a more predictable break in the middle of a busy day.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section-card">
  <h2>Your Day Flow</h2>
  <p class="section-intro">This is the cleanest version of the strategy you already discovered in practice.</p>

  <div class="timeline">
    <div class="timeline-step">
      <span class="time">Night Before</span>
      <strong>Lock your targets.</strong>
      Choose the park, verify tickets in the app, decide your first ride, and pick your likely first app move. Do not improvise this at the gate.
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-step">
      <span class="time">Arrival</span>
      <strong>Be there early enough to feel slightly ridiculous.</strong>
      That outside wait is usually cheaper than the extra 60 to 120 minutes you lose inside if you arrive casually.
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-step">
      <span class="time">Scan In</span>
      <strong>Split roles immediately.</strong>
      One person navigates to the first attraction. One person books the first available app service that supports your route.
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-step">
      <span class="time">First 2 Hours</span>
      <strong>Hit one headliner by standby and protect one later window through the app.</strong>
      This is where the day snowballs in your favor.
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-step">
      <span class="time">Lunch</span>
      <strong>Mobile order before you are hungry.</strong>
      Book the pickup window early, then confirm when you are nearby. This is one of the easiest ways to avoid a dumb, energy-draining line.
    </div>
    <div class="timeline-step">
      <span class="time">Afternoon</span>
      <strong>Spend your saved time intentionally.</strong>
      Use it on shows, kid zones, another major ride, or souvenir shopping instead of wandering into a surprise 80-minute queue.
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section-card">
  <h2>Sample Tokyo Disneyland Route</h2>
  <p class="section-intro">Based on your notes, this is a very solid family-friendly Disneyland flow, especially if your group wants a strong morning and a lighter afternoon.</p>

  <div class="grid cols-2">
    <div class="card accent-land">
      <h3>Morning attack plan</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Rope drop <strong>Enchanted Tale of Beauty and the Beast</strong> if it is your number-one priority.</li>
        <li>Use app benefits on a high-demand second target such as <strong>The Happy Ride with Baymax</strong>, <strong>Splash Mountain</strong>, <strong>Monsters, Inc. Ride &amp; Go Seek!</strong>, or <strong>Pooh’s Hunny Hunt</strong>, depending on availability.</li>
        <li>Fold in lower-wait fillers nearby such as <strong>Star Tours</strong>, <strong>Stitch Encounter</strong>, <strong>it’s a small world</strong>, the <strong>Castle Carrousel</strong>, or <strong>Alice’s Tea Party</strong>.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="card accent-gold">
      <h3>Afternoon glide path</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Schedule lunch with Mobile Order before noon rush.</li>
        <li>Use <strong>Entry Request</strong> for shows like <strong>Mickey’s Magical Music World</strong> when available.</li>
        <li>After lunch, pivot to <strong>Toontown</strong> for kids, <strong>Big Thunder Mountain</strong> for another big ride, then shop when lines are worst.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section id="parks" class="section-card">
  <h2>Choose Your Park Like a Strategist</h2>
  <p class="section-intro">Tokyo Disneyland is easier to optimize for a classic Disney day. Tokyo DisneySea is deeper, moodier, and usually better if your group wants a more distinctive park experience.</p>

  <div class="park-heading">
    <h3>Tokyo Disneyland</h3>
    <span class="tag land">Classic Magic</span>
  </div>
  <div class="grid cols-3">
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>Fantasyland / nearby</strong>
      Beauty and the Beast, Pooh’s Hunny Hunt, it’s a small world, Peter Pan’s Flight, Dumbo, and the castle-area classics make this the most important family rope-drop zone.
    </div>
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>Tomorrowland</strong>
      Baymax, Star Tours, and Stitch Encounter are strong morning pairings if your first route bends that direction.
    </div>
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>Westernland / Critter Country</strong>
      Big Thunder Mountain and Splash Mountain are major time-savers when you catch them through smart timing instead of brute-force lines.
    </div>
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>Toontown</strong>
      Great for a lower-stress afternoon with kids. Roger Rabbit’s Car Toon Spin is the attraction to watch here.
    </div>
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>Adventureland</strong>
      Jungle Cruise and Pirates make good secondary attractions once headliner pressure drops.
    </div>
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>World Bazaar</strong>
      Not the place to linger first thing. Save shopping for later unless weather forces a reset.
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="park-heading" style="margin-top: 28px;">
    <h3>Tokyo DisneySea</h3>
    <span class="tag sea">Best Atmosphere</span>
  </div>
  <div class="grid cols-3">
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>Fantasy Springs</strong>
      Anna and Elsa’s Frozen Journey, Rapunzel’s Lantern Festival, Peter Pan’s Never Land Adventure, and Fairy Tinker Bell’s Busy Buggies can shape the whole day if they are priorities.
    </div>
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>Mysterious Island</strong>
      Journey to the Center of the Earth and 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea are two of the park’s signature moves.
    </div>
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>American Waterfront</strong>
      Tower of Terror and Toy Story Mania! are major draws, with a completely different energy from Disneyland.
    </div>
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>Lost River Delta</strong>
      Indiana Jones Adventure and Raging Spirits are efficient targets for thrill-focused groups.
    </div>
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>Mediterranean Harbor</strong>
      Soaring: Fantastic Flight is one of the toughest waits in the resort. Treat it like a headliner, not a filler.
    </div>
    <div class="park-card">
      <strong>Mermaid Lagoon / Arabian Coast / Port Discovery</strong>
      Best zones for family pacing, weather cover, and lower-intensity attractions when your group needs a breather.
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section-card">
  <h2>Min-Max Rules of Thumb</h2>
  <div class="grid cols-2">
    <div class="card accent-gold">
      <h3>Worth paying for</h3>
      <p>Premier Access is most compelling when it protects a ride that is both popular and time-expensive. Beauty and the Beast, Baymax, Splash Mountain, Soaring, Tower of Terror, and major Fantasy Springs attractions are the usual mental category.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="card accent-sea">
      <h3>Usually worth doing free first</h3>
      <p>If a ride is available through Priority Pass or is simply low enough to knock out early, save your money and spend your planning discipline instead.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="card accent-land">
      <h3>Best time to shop</h3>
      <p>When the ride lines are ugly. Your opportunity cost is lowest in the middle of the afternoon or near your exit.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="card accent-gold">
      <h3>Best family reset</h3>
      <p>Mobile-order lunch, an indoor show, then a lower-intensity zone. This keeps kids from crashing right when the park is busiest.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
